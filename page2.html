Earthships

What is an Earthship?

An Earthship is an off-grid sustainable building made of natural and recycled materials. It is designed to be energy-efficient, self-sustaining, and use renewable resources such as solar, wind and water. Earthships are also designed to be able to withstand extreme weather conditions, such as hurricanes and earthquakes.

Pro's Con's
Low-Cost: Earthships are generally much cheaper to build than conventional homes, due to the use of recycled materials and the fact that they can be built with minimal labor.
Durable: Earthships are designed to be extremely durable and can last for many years without needing major repairs.
Self-Sufficient: Earthships are designed to be self-sufficient, meaning they can generate their own power and water supply as well as provide food for the inhabitants.
Environmentally Friendly: Earthships are designed with a minimal environmental impact and are often powered by renewable energy sources. 		Difficult to Build: Building an Earthship requires a great deal of knowledge and experience in construction, which can make it difficult for a novice to build one.
Not for Everyone: Earthships are not for everyone, as they require a certain level of commitment and dedication.
Not Always Legal: Depending on local regulations, building an Earthship may not be allowed in certain areas.
Maintenance: Earthships require a significant amount of maintenance and upkeep to keep them functioning properly.
